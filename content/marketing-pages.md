## What Your Plan Might Look Like

### Example: Tension Headache

**Context:** You've been staring at screens all day. Your neck and shoulders feel tight, and a dull ache is settling in.

**Rootwise Suggests:**

**Foods & Drinks:**
- Water with a pinch of sea salt and lemon (rehydration + minerals)
- Herbal teas: chamomile (calming), ginger (circulation), peppermint (soothing)
- Leafy greens, berries, walnuts (magnesium, antioxidants)
- Warm soups with magnesium-rich veggies

**Habits to Experiment With:**
- Step away from screens every 45 minutes
- Stretch your neck and shoulders gently
- Try a slow, 10-minute walk outdoors
- Dim lights and reduce sensory overload

**When to Talk to a Doctor:**
- Sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache
- Vision, speech, or balance changes
- Headache after injury or with fever and stiff neck

---

### Example: Bloating After Bread

**Context:** You notice uncomfortable bloating after eating bread or pasta. You want to explore what might help.

**Rootwise Suggests:**

**Foods & Drinks:**
- Warm lemon water with ginger (digestion support)
- Fermented veggies like sauerkraut or kimchi (probiotics)
- Low-FODMAP veggies cooked in olive oil
- Soaked chia pudding with cinnamon (gentle fiber)

**Habits to Experiment With:**
- Chew slowly and pause between bites
- Keep a gentle food journal for 1 week
- Relaxing breathwork before meals
- Consider timing: eat when calm, not rushed

**When to Talk to a Doctor:**
- Persistent abdominal pain or vomiting
- Blood in stool or black, tarry stool
- Rapid weight loss or severe fatigue

---

### Example: Restless Sleep

**Context:** You toss and turn at night, and your mind races with looping thoughts.

**Rootwise Suggests:**

**Foods & Drinks:**
- Warm oat milk with nutmeg or chamomile tea
- Protein + complex carbs at dinner (stable blood sugar)
- Magnesium-rich pumpkin seeds
- Limit caffeine after 2 pm

**Habits to Experiment With:**
- Dim lights one hour before bed
- Journaling to release looping thoughts
- Short yin stretches or legs-up-the-wall pose
- Cool, dark, quiet bedroom

**When to Talk to a Doctor:**
- Signs of sleep apnea (loud snoring, choking)
- Mood changes, depression, or anxiety spikes
- Sleep loss over 3 weeks impacting daily life

## Fast Experiments: Evidence-Informed Body Hacks

Rootwise sometimes suggests **quick, low-risk experiments** inspired by wellness research. These are not medical treatments‚Äîthey're gentle practices some people find helpful.

### Examples:

**Contrast Hydrotherapy for Tension Relief**  
Alternating warm and cool water on tense muscles may help circulation and relaxation. (Always test temperature safely; avoid extremes.)

**Box Breathing for Stress Response**  
Breathe in for 4 counts, hold for 4, exhale for 4, hold for 4. Repeat. This simple technique may help activate your parasympathetic nervous system.

**Oral Rehydration Concepts**  
Adding a pinch of salt and a squeeze of lemon to water mimics oral rehydration solutions, which may help with hydration, especially after exertion or mild dehydration.

**Important:** These suggestions are educational and exploratory‚Äînot prescriptions. If something feels wrong, stop and consult a professional.
